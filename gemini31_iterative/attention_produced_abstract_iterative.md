The Transformer is a novel architecture for sequence transduction that relies entirely on attention mechanisms, eliminating the need for recurrence or convolution. Its Multi-Head Attention allows the model to attend to information from different representation subspaces in parallel, offering superior computational efficiency and scalability. To handle sequence order, the model uses positional encodings, while residual connections and layer normalization ensure stable training. Beyond translation, the Transformer demonstrates strong generalization capabilities in English constituency parsing, outperforming previous models even in small-data regimes. Experimental results confirm that the architecture is robust, with performance sensitive to hyperparameter tuning but consistently improved by larger model sizes. Visualizations of the attention mechanism reveal that the model effectively captures long-distance dependencies and structural linguistic relationships. Ultimately, the Transformer achieves state-of-the-art results on WMT translation tasks while training significantly faster than traditional architectures. Future research aims to extend this approach to other modalities, such as images and audio, and to investigate more efficient, restricted attention mechanisms.
