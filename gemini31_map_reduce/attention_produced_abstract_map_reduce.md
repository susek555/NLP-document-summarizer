The Transformer is a novel neural network architecture that replaces traditional recurrent and convolutional layers with a self-attention mechanism, enabling superior parallelization and faster training. Relying entirely on multi-head self-attention, the model utilizes a stacked encoder-decoder structure featuring residual connections, layer normalization, and point-wise feed-forward networks. To maintain sequence order, the architecture incorporates sinusoidal positional encodings and learned embeddings. The model employs Scaled Dot-Product Attention to compute representations, while multi-head attention allows for simultaneous focus on different representation subspaces. Compared to RNNs and CNNs, the Transformer offers constant-time dependency operations and shorter paths for long-range dependencies. Trained on WMT 2014 datasets using Adam optimization, residual dropout, and label smoothing, the model achieved state-of-the-art performance in machine translation. Furthermore, it demonstrated strong results in English constituency parsing with minimal task-specific tuning. The authors conclude that the Transformer's efficiency and performance make it a robust framework for sequence modeling. Future research aims to extend this architecture to non-text modalities and improve efficiency for larger inputs.
